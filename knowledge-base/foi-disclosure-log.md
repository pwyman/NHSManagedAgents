# NHS Westshire ICB — FOI Disclosure Log

> **Purpose:** This log records all FOI and EIR requests received by NHS Westshire ICB and their outcomes. It is used by the FOI Agent to identify duplicate or similar previous requests and to inform draft responses. The ICB publishes a public version of this log on its website.

**Response types:**
- **DISCLOSED** — All requested information provided
- **PARTIAL** — Some information provided; remainder withheld under exemption
- **REFUSED** — Request refused under FOIA exemption(s)
- **NOT HELD** — Information not held by NHS Westshire ICB
- **REFERRED** — Requester directed to a published source (s.21)
- **TRANSFERRED** — Request transferred to another public authority

## FOI-2024-0005

**Date Received:** 28 May 2024  
**Date Answered:** 25 June 2024  
**Subject:** ICB sickness absence rate for 2023/24  
**Response Type:** DISCLOSED  

**Response:** The ICB's sickness absence rate for the financial year 2023/24 was 4.2%. This compares favourably with the NHS national average of 5.7% (NHS England Workforce Statistics, 2023/24). Short-term absence accounted for 2.8% and long-term absence (28 days or more) accounted for 1.4%. The most common reasons for sickness absence were: musculoskeletal (23%), anxiety/stress/depression (31%), other (46%). This information is also published in the ICB Annual Report.

## FOI-2024-0011

**Date Received:** 12 August 2024  
**Date Answered:** 9 September 2024  
**Subject:** Number of redundancies and voluntary exits following the 2022 ICB merger  
**Response Type:** DISCLOSED  

**Response:** Following the establishment of NHS Westshire ICB on 1 July 2022 (merging Westshire North CCG, Westshire South CCG, and Chipping Valley CCG), the following exits occurred during the transition period (July 2022 – March 2023): Compulsory redundancies: 0. Voluntary exits (including voluntary redundancy, mutual agreement, and early retirement): 47. Of the 47 voluntary exits, 31 received redundancy payments totalling £824,000, and 16 left under other arrangements. The ICB is satisfied that the exit payments represent value for money in the context of the efficiency savings achieved through the merger. Full details of exit payment bands are published in the ICB Annual Report per HM Treasury guidance.
