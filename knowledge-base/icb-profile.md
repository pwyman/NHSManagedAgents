## 4. Board Composition and Governance

### Board Members

The NHS Westshire ICB Board consists of:
- Chief Executive Officer (Sarah Thornton) — Accountable Officer
- Chair (Dr James Whitmore) — Non-Executive, Lay member
- Chief Finance Officer (Michael Chen) — Executive
- Chief Nursing Officer & Quality Lead (Dr Patricia Adeyemi) — Executive
- Medical Director (Dr Robert Ashford) — Executive
- Chief Digital & Information Officer (Helen Park) — Executive
- 6 Non-Executive Directors (NEDs) — including at least 2 Lay members
- 4 Clinical members (GPs or other clinicians from within the ICB area)

**Total Board membership: approximately 16 members**

### Board Meetings

- Regular Board meetings: held on the third Wednesday of each month, 10:00–13:00
- Public Board meetings: held quarterly in March, June, September, and December
- All public meeting agendas and papers published on ICB website at least 3 working days in advance
- Minutes of public meetings published on ICB website within 30 days of the meeting
- Committees of the Board (published on website): Audit, Risk and Assurance Committee; Quality and Safety Committee; Remuneration Committee; Finance Committee; Primary Care Commissioning Committee

### Declared Interests

The ICB maintains a Register of Interests for all Board members and relevant staff. The register is published annually on the ICB website and available at any time on request. Requests for the current register should be directed to: corporateaffairs@westshire.icb.nhs.uk

### Equality and Diversity

The ICB publishes an annual Workforce Equality and Diversity report on its website, required under the Public Sector Equality Duty (PSED), Equality Act 2010.

Current published figures (most recent annual report):
- Female representation at Band 8a and above: 54%
- BAME representation at Band 8a and above: 18%
- Gender pay gap report: published annually on the ICB website and gov.uk gender pay gap service
- Disability disclosure rate: 7.2% (note: likely to be an undercount due to non-disclosure)

The ICB participates in the annual NHS Staff Survey. Results are published by NHS England and on the ICB website.

## 7. Primary Care Networks

The ICB area includes 4 Primary Care Networks (PCNs):

| PCN Name | GPs | Registered Patients |
|---|---|---|
| Westminster Central PCN | 38 | 28,000 |
| Westminster North & Brackley Vale PCN | 41 | 34,000 |
| Chipping St Mary PCN | 29 | 22,000 |
| Sutton Common Rural PCN | 24 | 19,000 |
| **Total** | **132** | **103,000** |

Note: PCN patient figures relate to patients registered with practices within each PCN. Total registered patients across all PCNs (103,000) is less than total ICB population (521,000) because not all residents are registered with a GP in the ICB area, and figures reflect GP-registered patients rather than population census data.

## 10. Complaints

The ICB follows the NHS Complaints Regulations 2009 and NHS England guidance on complaints handling.

| Metric | Value | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Total formal complaints received | 214 | Last full year (2024/25) |
| Complaints upheld or partially upheld | 89 (42%) | Last full year |
| Average complaint resolution time | 23 working days | Last full year |
| Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman (PHSO) referrals | 7 | Last full year |

Detailed complaints statistics are published annually in the ICB Annual Report and on the ICB website. The ICB Complaints Policy is available on the website.

The NHS Complaints Manager is within the Patient Experience and Quality team. Complaints about primary care (GP practices, dentists, pharmacies) are handled differently — see the ICB website for guidance.
